The honest version

Every accounting tool pretends to be simple.
None of them are.

QuickBooks costs $180/month, has 14 onboarding screens, and still requires you to know the difference between a journal entry and a ledger. Xero is barely different. Wave is "free" until you need anything that actually matters.

I built LedgerAI because I'm an accounting student who actually uses these tools professionally — and I was embarrassed that my clients, my family, and my friends couldn't use any of them without calling me first.

The goal: drop a CSV, get reports. Zero accounting knowledge required. The AI handles everything else.

The why

I'm a 3rd-year accounting student
with zero software background.
This is what I built anyway.

I'm not a developer. I've never taken a CS course. I built LedgerAI entirely because I was frustrated — frustrated at my audit internship watching senior accountants manually compile the same working papers every engagement, and frustrated at my bookkeeping job entering the same transactions into a spreadsheet that could have been automated.

The tools that exist are either too simple or too expensive. QuickBooks for a freelancer is like buying a forklift to move a couch. Wave and FreshBooks are too basic to actually replace an accountant. Nothing was built for the person who is serious about their finances but doesn't have a CPA designation.

So I used every tool available — Claude AI, modern web APIs, hours of accounting textbooks — and built the gap that exists in this market. Audit-grade output. Zero accounting background required.
